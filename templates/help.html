Error Messages


Error messages are either specific about the issue or "Something went wrong!". When an error message shows up as text on the screen, clicking on it will take you to the part of the help page that can be of assistance.


When an error message pops up as a screen alert, please contact the developer stating what led to it.



Registering


Usernames must be 1-25 characters long, alphabets and digits are allowed. Special characters: "-" "_" "." are also allowed but you may not start your username with special characters or have them come one after the other in your username, you may end the username with a special character.
Your CGPA is to be inputed as a number (decimals are allowed).
Passwords must be 8 or more characters long, including at least one digit.


Character limits: Fullname(70), Username(25), School(50), Major(100), StudentID(30).
Click on login if you have already registered to take you to the login page.




Logging In


TSC is case insensitive, so your username doesn't have to be inputted the exact way you registered for the log in to work.
We take care of whitespaces for you but try not to include them in your username. Whitespaces: "Obafemi ". No Whitespace: "Obafemi".


Click on register or sign up if you don't have an account already.




Logging Out


To log out, open the User dropdown on the navigation bar and click on log out.


For mobile view, you need to tap on the hamburger to get the navigation bar to show.




Accessing About TSC


To access the carousel, click on TheStudentCompanion on the left side of the navigation bar


Then scroll down to get the carousel (slideshow). On the last slide, there's a link to register or the hlep page, depending on if the user is logged in or not.




The Navigation Bar


To open the dropdowns, click on the elements with downward arrows next to them. The current page will have a different look to the others on the navigation bar, just to keep extra track of where you are.


For desktop users, there's a hover effect on the elements. To achieve his hover on modile, hold down on the element but don't tap on it.




Home


The home page displays classes for that day, all tasks with an urgent statu and the next three assessments.
The home page has three active links. Clicking on: "Classes Today" will take you to your timetable. || "Urgent Tasks" will take you to your todo list. || "Upcoming Assessments" will take you to your assessments.





Courses


Clicking on Red Circles: "Course Code" will take you to the outline page with no outline open. || "Course" will take you to your ToDo page. || "Units" will take you to the assessment page. || "✎" will take you to your timetable.
Clicking on Purple Circles: These perform the same function, the difference is the specificity. They take you to the same pages but with information on the particular course that was clicked on through its code, name or units.


Two courses may not have the same course code.




Courses: Add, Edit and Delete


To add a new course, click on the "Add a New Course" button which turns into a cancel button after it is clicked (as seen in the picture). If you no longer wish to add a new course, simply click on cancel.
To edit a course, click on the meatballs menu on its row to reveal the edit and delete buttons, then click on whichever one you wish to do.

Character limits: Course Name(200), Course Code(15).




Course Outlines


You can either access a course's outline through the courses page or by selecting from the Course Code menu (top left) and clicking on "Check Outline". Topics can have three statuses: "Done", "Undone" or "Urgent". That's left to the user.


Topics are arranged in order of the weeks. From earliest to latest.




Outlines: Add, Status, Edit and Delete


To add a topic to an outline, the course outline has to be open. After opening the outline, click on the "Add a new topic" button (it turns to a cancel button after it is clicked).
To change the status of a topic, click on the status button.
To edit or delete a topic, click on the meatballs menu to show to edit and delete(✘) buttons.
To delete the entire course outline, click on the delete button at the top right side of the screen.

Character limits: Topic(200), Topic Details(400). The status button is "Undone" by default, it goes from Undone -> Urgent -> Done -> Undone.




Timetable


This simply displays your class schedule.


No measures have been put in place to ensure that the classes end before they start according to user input. If the user's class ends before it starts, that's fine by us :)




Timetable: Add, Edit and Delete


To add a new class, click on the "Add a new class" button (which turns into a cancel button after it is clicked).
To edit a class, click on the meatballs menu to show the edit and delete(✘) buttons.

Character limits: Venue(30).




To-Do


The to-do list comes with a filter, you can choose to see all tasks, the academic tasks or the non-academic tasks.
The to-do list has three status options as well: Undone, Done and Urgent.


Filtered views come with filtered options to add. You can't add a non-academic task while in the academic task filtered view and vice versa.




To-Do: Add, Status, Edit and Delete


To add a new task, click on the "Add a new ..." button for either academic or non-academic tasks. (The buttons become cancel buttons after they are clicked).
To change the status of a task, click on the status button.
To edit or delete a task, click on the meatballs menu to show the edit and delete(✘) buttons.

Character Limits: Task(300). The status button is "Undone" by default, it goes from Undone -> Urgent -> Done -> Undone.




Assessments


The assessments page also comes with a filter for mid-semester and exams(finals) but it only has two status options: Done and Undone.

In the brackets, (MS) means Mid-Semester and (E) means Examination.




Assessment: Add, Status, Edit and Delete


To add a new assessment, click on the "Add a new ..." button for either mid-semesters or exams. (The buttons become cancel buttons after they are clicked).
To change the status of an assessment, click on the status button.
To edit or delete an assessment, click on the meatballs menu to show the edit and delete(✘) buttons.

Character limits: Venue(50). The status button is "Undone" by default, it goes from Undone -> Done -> Undone.




Deleting Multiple


To access the deletion page, open the user dropdown and click on "Mass Deletion".
The mass deletion page allows you to delete multiple entries at once. This would most likely come in handy at the end of semesters or session.

If you choose to delete everything. Clicking on "Return to Home Page" will send you back to the home page.




Profile


To view your profile, open the User dropdown and click on profile.

From inside the profile page, you can update your profile, change your password, change the theme of your account and deactivate your account.




Update your Profile


To update your profile, first open the profile page then click on update profile. To submit, click on update.

Character limits: Fullname(70), Username(25), School(50), Major(100), StudentID(30).
The constraints in updating your profile are the same as those in registering your account.




Change your password


To change your password, first, open the profile page. Then click on Change Password.

The old password is required for this to work.




Theme


To change your theme, open the user dropdown, access to profile page. Then click on "Theme" and select whatever palette you want.

You have 10 theme options to pick from.




Deactivating your account.


From your profile page, you can deactivate your account. Deactivation here works manually. After deactivating, you have the option to reactivate or permanently delete your account.
To do this, log in after deactivating and you will be directed to the deactivation screen where you can make your choice by clciking on either side of the screen.

Permanently deleting your account also deletes all of your account's information.
